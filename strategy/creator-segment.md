# Creator Segment

This document defines target creator segments for StyleOS Creator Alpha and future Creator Studio Lite.

## 1. Outfit Creator

- Pain points: consultation arrives through scattered messages; outfit advice is hard to package; repeated questions consume time.
- Current workflow: posts outfit content, replies to private questions, may sell informal diagnosis.
- What StyleOS solves: intake form, outfit tags, rule cards, lite reports, shopping keyword cards.
- Why they may pay: saves time, improves delivery consistency, supports paid outfit diagnosis.
- What data they can contribute: abstracted outfit rules, synthetic cases, scenario feedback.
- What they should not upload publicly: real wardrobe photos, body measurements, contacts, private chat logs.

## 2. Hairstyle Creator

- Pain points: fans ask "what hairstyle suits me" but inputs are incomplete; barber communication is difficult.
- Current workflow: comments on face shape, hair volume, reference images, and salon advice.
- What StyleOS solves: face-shape tags, hair attribute tags, hairstyle rule cards, barber brief card.
- Why they may pay: can sell hairstyle suitability cards and salon communication briefs.
- What data they can contribute: abstracted hairstyle rules and synthetic barber brief examples.
- What they should not upload publicly: real face photos, scalp photos, salon records, private user images.

## 3. Makeup Creator

- Pain points: followers ask for product lists before makeup direction is clear.
- Current workflow: recommends looks, color directions, and product routines.
- What StyleOS solves: makeup taxonomy, color direction, makeup artist brief, daily / professional / camera scenarios.
- Why they may pay: can turn advice into paid makeup color cards and creator-branded reports.
- What data they can contribute: abstracted makeup direction rules and synthetic routine examples.
- What they should not upload publicly: bare-face photos, skin details, purchase histories, private health information.

## 4. Image Consultant

- Pain points: service delivery depends heavily on manual documents and one-off judgment.
- Current workflow: consultation, diagnosis, report writing, follow-up.
- What StyleOS solves: full style passport structure, scenario tags, rule cards, evidence levels.
- Why they may pay: improves service standardization and supports higher-value packages.
- What data they can contribute: anonymized feedback summaries and rule abstractions.
- What they should not upload publicly: real client profiles, identifiable cases, private reports.

## 5. Photography / Styling Studio

- Pain points: clients arrive unprepared for shoots; styling and makeup briefs are unclear.
- Current workflow: moodboard, makeup, outfit selection, shoot execution.
- What StyleOS solves: photo shoot brief, upper-body priority, camera color rules, execution cards.
- Why they may pay: improves pre-shoot preparation and reduces communication cost.
- What data they can contribute: synthetic shoot brief examples and scenario rules.
- What they should not upload publicly: client photos, shoot contracts, private galleries, real booking records.

## 6. Career Image Creator

- Pain points: professional image advice must balance credibility, approachability, and industry context.
- Current workflow: career coaching plus style suggestions.
- What StyleOS solves: client meeting rules, interview scenario tags, professional image reports.
- Why they may pay: can add a professional image module to existing coaching services.
- What data they can contribute: abstracted career image rules and synthetic meeting scenarios.
- What they should not upload publicly: employer names, interview records, client resumes, private career data.

## 7. Personal IP Coach

- Pain points: personal brand advice needs repeatable visual cues without losing credibility.
- Current workflow: positioning, content strategy, profile photos, visual identity advice.
- What StyleOS solves: content creator scenario, photo shoot brief, memorable cue strategy.
- Why they may pay: supports branded reports and repeatable visual identity packages.
- What data they can contribute: synthetic personal IP workflows and visual cue rules.
- What they should not upload publicly: real account handles, analytics screenshots, client brand strategy documents.
