# Haircut Taxonomy

This taxonomy defines starter haircut directions without assigning them to a fixed gender.

## `short_crop`

- Definition: Very short, clean shape close to the head.
- Useful signals: high need for low maintenance, strong preference for clarity.
- Common risks: may expose head shape, hairline, or crown flatness more clearly.
- Typical scenarios: workday, active lifestyle, strong refresh.
- Creator notes: confirm willingness to cut short and upkeep frequency.

## `pixie_inspired_short_cut`

- Definition: Short cut with lighter texture and visible shape variation.
- Useful signals: user accepts visible change and wants a sharper or more expressive direction.
- Common risks: can require frequent trims and careful execution.
- Typical scenarios: creator identity, strong personal style, photo profile.
- Creator notes: avoid gendered assumptions; focus on maintenance and face-frame balance.

## `bob`

- Definition: Short-to-chin or chin-adjacent structured cut.
- Useful signals: user wants clean outline and controlled length.
- Common risks: length may emphasize jawline if it stops at the widest point.
- Typical scenarios: daily upgrade, professional scene.
- Creator notes: check jawline, cheekbone, hair volume, and length landing point.

## `lob`

- Definition: Long bob, usually around collarbone or upper shoulder.
- Useful signals: user wants a transition option without cutting too short.
- Common risks: ends can become heavy or flip outward if not layered.
- Typical scenarios: workday, low-to-medium risk change.
- Creator notes: define layer and weight control clearly.

## `medium_layered_cut`

- Definition: Medium length with visible but controlled layers.
- Useful signals: need for movement, face framing, or weight control.
- Common risks: too many layers may increase maintenance or frizz.
- Typical scenarios: daily commute, client-facing work.
- Creator notes: connect layer height to face signals and maintenance.

## `long_layered_cut`

- Definition: Long hair with controlled layers for movement or weight release.
- Useful signals: user wants to keep length while reducing heaviness.
- Common risks: layers may look sparse on low-volume hair.
- Typical scenarios: low-risk change, creator content, daily styling.
- Creator notes: confirm hair density and styling time.

## `straight_long_hair`

- Definition: Long hair with straight, clean visual direction.
- Useful signals: user wants calm, minimal, or polished line.
- Common risks: may emphasize flat crown or face length when close to the scalp.
- Typical scenarios: daily, professional, minimal style.
- Creator notes: consider crown support and face-framing options.

## `soft_wave`

- Definition: Gentle wave with low-to-medium structure.
- Useful signals: user wants softness, movement, or less hard outline.
- Common risks: may collapse on low-hold hair or require styling tools.
- Typical scenarios: date, daily upgrade, photo profile.
- Creator notes: state maintenance and heat-tool needs.

## `structured_wave`

- Definition: More defined wave with clearer shape and presence.
- Useful signals: user wants stronger visual presence or camera readability.
- Common risks: may feel high maintenance or too styled for conservative contexts.
- Typical scenarios: content creation, photoshoot, event.
- Creator notes: clarify whether this is daily or occasion-only.

## `side_part_style`

- Definition: Parting direction that shifts visual weight to one side.
- Useful signals: asymmetry balance, forehead balance, softer transition.
- Common risks: can over-cover one side or create shadow near the face.
- Typical scenarios: workday, client meeting, soft professional.
- Creator notes: define how much coverage is intended.

## `center_part_style`

- Definition: Balanced middle parting direction.
- Useful signals: stable symmetry, clean long hair direction.
- Common risks: may emphasize face length, flat crown, or strict symmetry.
- Typical scenarios: daily, polished, minimal.
- Creator notes: not a default for every long-hair user.

## `tied_updo_style`

- Definition: Hair tied, half-tied, clipped, or updo direction.
- Useful signals: workplace cleanliness, camera stability, active day.
- Common risks: can expose hairline, forehead, or jawline more directly.
- Typical scenarios: workday, public speaking, photoshoot.
- Creator notes: pair with hairline and face-framing notes.

## `clean_short_haircut`

- Definition: Short haircut focused on neatness, edge control, and low noise.
- Useful signals: professional scene, low maintenance, client-facing work.
- Common risks: may feel too plain if user wants memorability.
- Typical scenarios: client meeting, interview, daily commute.
- Creator notes: add one optional personal cue when needed.

## `textured_short_haircut`

- Definition: Short haircut with visible texture, movement, or separation.
- Useful signals: user wants energy, creator identity, or less rigid short hair.
- Common risks: may require product, trims, or morning styling.
- Typical scenarios: creator content, casual work, personal IP.
- Creator notes: clarify styling-tool willingness.
